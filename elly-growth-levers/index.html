Key Growth Levers to Scale EllyFeelGood Products, Services & Mission

Solidifying the Current Market for Growth

Introduction

As part of our independent initiative to support emerging local businesses, we have conducted this review proactively, without any expectation or obligation. Our goal is to present insights and strategic growth levers for EllyFeelGood—shared in good faith—to inform discussion and decision‑making.

Agenda

  • Current Digital Footprint & Channels
  • Tracking & Data Infrastructure
  • Customer Communication & CRM Gaps
  • Opportunities: WhatsApp & Email Automation
  • Actionable Growth Levers
  • Roadmap & Next Steps

Current Digital Footprint

  • Website powered by WordPress, using Avada theme
  • Forms and newsletter captures via Fluent Forms plugin
  • Google Analytics 4 tracking through MonsterInsights
  • SEO handled using Yoast
  • Anti‑spam via CleanTalk plugin

Engagement Channels & Content

  • WhatsApp link for direct communication
  • Email for contact and newsletter via forms
  • Social media presence (Facebook, Instagram)
  • Static content, calls‑to‑action, minimal dynamic personalization yet

Tracking & Data Infrastructure

  • GA4 in place with MonsterInsights plugin
  • Google Tag Manager referenced but usage unclear
  • Missing: conversion funnel metrics, detailed form event tracking
  • Limited attribution tracking for channels like WhatsApp or social

Customer Communication Pathways

  • Website contact form → Fluent Forms → internal handling
  • Newsletter signup form (“Join Our Family”) via Fluent Forms
  • WhatsApp link triggers manual messaging sessions
  • No observed automation, lead scoring, or multi‑channel integration yet

CRM & Automation Readiness

  • No visible evidence of external CRM (e.g. HubSpot, Salesforce, Mailchimp CRM)
  • Forms do not appear to send data to external services or via webhooks
  • Channels seem siloed (WhatsApp, forms, email) without unified flow
  • Core systems appear relatively simple — positive foundation for automation

Opportunities: WhatsApp as Sales Channel

  • Upgrade to WhatsApp Business or Business API
  • Deploy bots for lead qualification, auto‑responses, FAQs
  • Track WhatsApp leads in CRM; analyze conversion rates
  • Use WhatsApp for promotions, broadcast messages, product launches

Opportunities: Email Marketing & Lead Nurturing

  • Connect forms to an ESP / CRM to automate welcome & nurture flows
  • Segment leads by source (form, WhatsApp, newsletter)
  • Create content flows (tips, product education, promotions)
  • Use A/B testing and optimize subject lines, send schedule, copy

Data & Analytics Gaps & Recommendations

  • Implement event tracking for form submissions, WhatsApp clicks, CTA interactions
  • Define conversion funnel and set up goals in GA4 or GTM
  • Add pixels/tags for social platforms for paid and retargeting campaigns
  • Establish dashboards that unify traffic, leads & conversions by channel

Growth Levers: Prioritized Action Plan

  • Short‑term: Tighten tracking / integrate ESP / connect WhatsApp better
  • Medium‑term: Automate lead nurturing, segment customers, enrich content
  • Long‑term: Scale omnichannel automation, predictive analytics, personalization

Roadmap & Next Steps

  • Confirm current systems (CRM / ESP) and ownership of analytics access
  • Set up event + funnel tracking; define KPIs
  • Evaluate ESP / CRM options & integration cost
  • Design WhatsApp auto response / bot flow & pilot
  • Implement email nurture series, test content strategy
  • Review progress monthly; refine based on data

Q&A & Discussion

We welcome questions, feedback, and suggestions. Let’s align on which levers to prioritize.
